Visualizing and Comparing Market Caps of Major, Publicly Traded US-Based Companies

The bar chart below represents the market caps for the largest companies traded publicly in the S&P 500. The list of companies is composed of the top 5 technology based companies and the top 5 non technology based companies. The ’ticker’ dropdown also includes the options ’Tech Industry’ and ’Other Industries’. These market caps are summed and divided by the number of companies (5) to show the mean market cap data.
The Other Industries are representing the top 5 non tech companies, not the entire space of non tech companies. In other visualizations, tech industries only make up about 30% of the S&P 500 since there are many more non tech companies.

Tech Companies

AAPL (Apple Inc.): Apple designs and sells consumer electronics like the iPhone, Mac, and iPad, as well as software and digital services such as the App Store and iCloud.

AMZN (Amazon.com Inc.): Amazon is a global e-commerce and cloud computing giant, known for its online retail marketplace and AWS (Amazon Web Services) platform.

AVGO (Broadcom Inc.): Broadcom designs, develops, and supplies semiconductors and infrastructure software solutions for data centers, networking, and mobile devices.

GOOG (Alphabet Inc.): Alphabet is the parent company of Google, offering products in search, advertising, cloud computing, software, and hardware, including YouTube and Android.

MSFT (Microsoft Corp.): Microsoft develops software (like Windows and Office), cloud services (Azure), and hardware products, and owns LinkedIn and Xbox.

Other Companies

BRK (Berkshire Hathaway Inc.): Led by Warren Buffett, Berkshire Hathaway is a diversified holding company with businesses in insurance, railroads, utilities, and equity investments in major firms.

JPM (JPMorgan Chase & Co.): JPMorgan Chase is one of the largest global financial services firms, providing banking, investment, and asset management services.

LLY (Eli Lilly and Co.): Eli Lilly is a major pharmaceutical company focused on developing medicines for diabetes, cancer, mental health, and autoimmune diseases.

NVDA (NVIDIA Corp.): NVIDIA designs GPUs used in gaming, data centers, AI, and autonomous vehicles, and is a leader in AI hardware and software platforms.

WMT (Walmart Inc.): Walmart is the world’s largest retailer, operating a global chain of discount department stores and grocery supercenters.
